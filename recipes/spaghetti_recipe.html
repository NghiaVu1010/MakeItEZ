Spaghetti Code

Prep Time: 40 mins.
Submitted by: G.R.

It's really easy to make spaghetti at home! Using jarred tomato sauce means you can have dinner on the table in under a half hour, too. Keep on reading to find out how to make a basic spaghetti with meat sauce!

# Steps Notes
1 Heat oven to 350°.  
2 Mix all ingredients. Shape mixture into 1 1/2-inch balls.  
3 Place in ungreased jelly roll pan, 15 1/2x10 1/2x1 inch.  
4 Bake uncovered or until beef is no longer pink in center and juice is clear. 15 to 20 minutes
5 Meanwhile, heat oil in Dutch oven over medium-high heat. Cook onion and garlic in oil until onion is tender. Stir occasionally
6 Stir in remaining Sauce ingredients except spaghetti and cheese, breaking up tomatoes.  
7 Heat to boiling; reduce heat to low. Cover and simmer 30 minutes. Stir occasionally
8 Add meatballs to sauce. Cover and simmer 15 minutes.  
9 Cook and drain spaghetti as directed on package. Place spaghetti on large platter. Top with meatballs and sauce. Sprinkle with cheese.  

You can shape meatballs fast by using an ice-cream scoop. Or instead, pat the mixture into a 9x3-inch rectangle in an ungreased 13x9-inch pan, cut into squares and separate slightly. Then, bake uncovered 25-30 minutes until no longer pink in center and meat thermometer inserted in center reads 160°F.

Spaghetti and Meatballs -- nearly everyone's favorite "Italian" dish -- is truly international fare. Spaghetti originated in the Orient, meatballs in the Middle East, tomato sauce in Mexico, and the essential Parmesan cheese really does come from Italy--it has been produced in the city of Parma for more than 800 years!

Ingredients:

Meatballs
# Item Notes
1 1/2 pound ground beef  
3/4 cup dry bread crumbs  
1/2 cup milk  
2 tablespoons grated Parmesan cheese  
1 tablespoon chopped fresh parsley  
1 teaspoon salt  
1/2 teaspoon dried oregano leaves  
1/4 teaspoon pepper  
1 medium onion finely chopped (1/2 cup)
1 egg  
Sauce
# Item Notes
1 tablespoon olive or vegetable oil  
1 medium onion chopped (1/2 cup)
1 clove garlic finely chopped
1 can (28 oz) whole tomatoes undrained
1 can (6 oz) tomato paste  
1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley  
1/4 cup water  
1 teaspoon sugar  
1 teaspoon salt  
1/2 teaspoon dried basil leaves  
1 package (16 oz) spaghetti  
- grated parmesan cheese to preference
